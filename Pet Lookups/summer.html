Imagine right now,
being laid on a shore,
a soft fluffy towel,
set out on the floor.

Your fingers create swirls,
in the warm grains of sand,
The sun like a gentle caress,
skin growing ever more tanned.

Nostrils are overwhelmed,
smelling the salty sea air,
and grease from the donut stalls,
teasing you from the funfair.

Feel the gentle shanty breeze,
tickling strands of your hair.
Blowing them up into the wind,
and still, I forget to care.

Seagulls screeching their songs,
fighting over chip scraps,
winning their fight, taking flight,
over peoples heads in caps.

The sea glistening from afar,
waves clapping for all to hear,
kids screaming, enjoying the summer,
with the people whom they hold dear.

Whether it be a splodge in the ocean,
or a game of rounders or two,
a sandcastle, a game of catch,
time with the family or crew.
For these are the memories,
you will always hold close to heart,
they bring warmth to the coldest of days,
or when families are torn apart.

Memorial pet for my dad,
miss you forever wink.
